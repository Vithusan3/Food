Recipe:-

SRI LANKAN FISH CUTLETS

One of my absolutely favourite things to eat
are hot hot fish cutlets, ideally fried in coconut
oil. It’s a bit of a process to make (at least for me),
so we don’t make them often. I have had a hankering for homemade
fish cutlets for a while now, but with curfew in place,
and limited access to produce, I’ve been reluctant
to use precious ingredients unnecessarily

  • 1 Mackerel tin
  • 500g boiled Potatoes
  • 2 Onions
  • Curry leaves
  • Coriander leaves
  • Green chillies
  • Tomato sauce
  • A squeeze of Lime
  • Chilli powder, Pepper, Salt
  • Flour slurry for dipping*
  • Homemade Biscuit crumbs

Instructions:

  1. Cook the fish: Boil or steam the fish until fully cooked. Add a pinch of salt and pepper for flavor.
  2. Prepare the mixture: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flaked fish, mashed potatoes, chopped onion, cilantro, garlic, ginger, garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Shape into cutlets: Take a small portion of the mixture and shape it into a cutlet or patty.
  4. Coat with breadcrumbs: Dip each cutlet into the beaten eggs and then coat with breadcrumbs.
  5. Fry the cutlets: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Fry the cutlets until golden brown on both sides (2-3 minutes per side).
  6. Drain excess oil: Place the fried cutlets on a paper towel to absorb any excess oil.
  7. Serve: Serve the fish cutlets hot with your favorite sauce or chutney.
