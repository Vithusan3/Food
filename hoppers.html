Egg Hoppers Recipe

Egg Hoppers for Breakfast - Lavender and Lovage
Egg Hoppers are a popular breakfast dish in Sri Lanka,
where they are served with or without eggs, but
nearly alwaysbr kiribath-with-lunu-miris-coconut-rice-withbr
sambols, curry, dhal and coconut gravy.

  • 2 cups raw rice
  • 1/2 cup cooked rice
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast (optional, for fermentation)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water (for grinding)
  • Eggs (as needed)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green chilies and fresh coriander (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Batter:
    1. Rinse the raw rice and soak it in water for about 4-6 hours or overnight.
    2. In a blender, combine soaked raw rice, cooked rice, grated coconut, active dry yeast (if using), sugar, and salt.
    3. Add water gradually and blend until you get a smooth batter. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter.
    4. Allow the batter to ferment for 6-8 hours or overnight. If using yeast, the batter should rise.
  2. Cook the Hoppers:
    1. Heat an appam pan or a small non-stick pan over medium heat.
    2. Pour a ladle of the batter into the center of the pan and quickly swirl it around to coat the edges. The center should be thicker than the edges.
    3. Crack an egg into the center of the batter. If you prefer, you can beat the egg slightly before pouring it for a more uniform texture.
    4. Cover the pan with a lid and cook until the edges are crispy, and the egg is cooked to your liking. You can cook it with a runny yolk or fully cooked, depending on your preference.
    5. Repeat the process for the remaining batter and eggs.
