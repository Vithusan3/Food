Kottu

A popular and flavorful Sri Lankan street food dish.
It consists of shredded flatbread (roti) stir-fried with a combination of ingredients
including chicken, vegetables, eggs, and an assortment of spices and sauces.

  • 4-5 parottas (store-bought or homemade), shredded
  • 1 lb (450g) boneless chicken, cut into small pieces
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 cup cabbage, finely chopped
  • 1 cup carrots, grated
  • 1 cup leeks or spring onions, chopped
  • 2 eggs (optional)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Roti: Shred the parottas or flatbreads into small pieces. You can use a knife or your hands to do this.
  2. Cook Chicken Curry: In a pan, heat oil and sauté chopped onions until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute. Add chicken pieces, chili powder, turmeric powder, curry powder, and salt. Cook until the chicken is fully cooked. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and the mixture becomes a thick curry.
  3. Prepare Vegetables: In a separate pan, heat oil. Add chopped cabbage, grated carrots, and leeks or spring onions. Stir-fry until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Assemble Kottu: Add the shredded parottas to the vegetable mixture. Pour soy sauce and ketchup over the mixture. Optionally, you can push the mixture to the side of the pan and scramble eggs on the other side. Mix the eggs into the rest of the mixture. Add the cooked chicken curry to the pan. Sprinkle black pepper and salt to taste. Using two flat spatulas, chop and mix everything together on the griddle until well combined. The chopping sound is characteristic of kottu preparation.
  5. Serve: Once everything is well mixed and heated through, remove from heat. Serve hot with a side of raita or your favorite chutney.
